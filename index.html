Trung Vu

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

My name is Trung Vu. I am currently a second-year mathematics Ph.D. student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I obtained my BA in mathematics with a concentration in neuroscience from St. Olaf College

I am interested in algebraic combinatorics and combinatorial aspects of representation theory and mathematical physics. Specifically, I like to think about cluster algebra, integrable systems, vertex model and vertex algebra. Currently, I am studying the T-system, Q-system and domino tiling of Aztec diamond and the artic curve phenomenon. I put down some short introductory ideas from my own notes to each of these topics on the "Topics" tab above along with references for more details.

Recently, I started exploring the realm of quantum computing and its applications to quantum many-body problems. My hope is that quantum computing can help solving integrability and exactly solvability problems in statistical mechanics. After all, a quantum computer is an integrable system itself (check out here). The idea of simulating a quantum many-body system by using another quantum system dates back to Richard Feynman but it was not until recently that some significant progress has been made (check out here)
